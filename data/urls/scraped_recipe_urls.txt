=== Creamy Red Lentil Soup ===
Servings: 5
Difficulty: Easy
Favorite: No
Link: https://reciperunner.com/creamy-red-lentil-soup/
Image: data/urls/img/Creamy_Red_Lentil_Soup_ced80dca.jpg

[1]
[0.2 tbsp] olive oil
[0.2 cup] yellow onion, diced
[0.2 cup] carrots, diced
[0.2 cup] celery, diced
[0.4] cloves of garlic, grated
[0.2 tbsp] tomato paste
[0.2 tbsp] smoked paprika
[0.2 tsp] ground cumin
[0.1 tsp] ground ginger
[0.1 tsp] turmeric, ground
[0.05 tsp] cinnamon, ground
[0.3 tsp] sriracha, optional
[0.3 cup] red lentils, rinsed
[0.6 tbsp] cilantro, chopped
[0.8 cup] vegetable broth, low sodium
[0.3 cup] water
[0.1 cup] coconut, canned milk optional use more water or broth in it’s place
[0.4] -4 tbsp fresh lemon juice

[Curry Cashews (optional)]
[0.1 cup] cashews, raw
[0.1 tsp] olive oil
[0.05 tsp] curry powder
[0.05 tsp] smoked paprika

Steps:
Curry Cashews (optional)
1. In a small bowl combine the oil, spices, salt and pepper. Add in the cashews and stir them until coated in the spice mixture. Add the cashew to a skillet heated over mediumlow heat. Let them toast for 35 minutes stirring them occasionally until they're golden brown and fragrant. Remove from the heat and set aside.
Creamy Red Lentil Soup
2. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over mediumhigh heat. When the oil is hot add in the onion, celery, and carrots. Season with salt and pepper. Sauté for 34 minutes. Add in the garlic, tomato paste, sriracha, and spices and sauté another minute.
3. Add in the red lentils and cilantro and pour in the broth and water. Stir everything together and bring the soup to a boil. Once boiling lower the heat to mediumlow and cover with a lid. Simmer for 1520 minutes or until the lentils are tender and starting to break apart.
4. Turn off the heat and use an immersion stick blender to purée the soup to your desired consistency. Pour in the coconut milk and lemon juice and stir until combined. Taste for seasoning and add more salt and pepper as needed. Garnish the soup with the curry cashews, cilantro, and extra sriracha if desired.


=== Mediterranean Eggplant and Halloumi Bowls with Lemon-Dill Sauce ===
Servings: 4
Difficulty: Easy
Favorite: No
Link: https://dishingouthealth.com/mediterranean-eggplant-and-halloumi-bowls-with-lemon-dill-sauce/
Image: data/urls/img/Mediterranean_Eggplant_and_Halloumi_Bowls_with_Lem_b7e39e34.jpg

[1]
[1.5 tbsp] olive oil, extra-virgin divided
[0.25] eggplant, small-medium globe cut into 1/2-inch cubes
[0.25 tbsp] tamari
[2 oz] halloumi cheese, cut into 1/3-in.-thick slices
[0.5 cup] cherry tomatoes, halved
[0.75] cucumbers, Persian thinly sliced (or 1/2 English cucumber)
[0.25 tbsp] capers, roughly chopped
[0.5 cup] quinoa, cooked grains of choice (farro rice or barley) or arugula for lower carb

[Lemon-Dill Yogurt Sauce]
[0.12 cup] yogurt, plain whole-milk Greek
[0.5 tbsp] lemon, fresh juice
[0.5 tbsp] fresh dill, finely chopped plus more for garnish
[0.5] garlic, grated cloves
[0.25] to 2 tbsp. olive oil, extra-virgin

Steps:
1. In a medium bowl, combine tomatoes, cucumbers, capers, and 2 Tbsp. olive oil. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
2. In a separate bowl, prepare LemonDill Yogurt Sauce:Combine yogurt, lemon juice, dill, garlic and 1 to 2 Tbsp. extravirgin olive oil; whisk to combine. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
3. Heat 2 Tbsp. olive oil in a skillet over mediumhigh. Once hot, add eggplant and cook for 6 to 8 minutes, until nicely seared. Add tamari and cook 2 more minutes. Transfer eggplant to a bowl.Add remaining 2 Tbsp. oil to pan. Arrange halloumi slices in pan and cook until deeply golden, about 2 minutes per side. Watch closely to avoid burning or melting.Divide grain of choice (or arugula) evenly into four bowls. Top with sautéed eggplant, halloumi cheese, and a few spoonfuls of tomatocucumber salad. Add a big dollop of LemonDill Yogurt Sauce, and garnish with extra fresh dill and cracked black pepper.

Notes:
MakeAhead: The lemondill sauce can be prepared up to 3 days ahead. Refrigerate in an airtight jar or container. You can also prepare the tomatocucumber salad up to 1 day ahead.
Store: Store grains, eggplant, and halloumi separately from salad and sauce.
Reheat: Reheat grain, eggplant, and halloumi mixture in the microwave or in a skillet over medium heat. Add tomatocucumber salad and sauce after bowl is warm.
