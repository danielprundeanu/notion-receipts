=== Blackberry Pancakes ===
Servings: None
Difficulty: Easy
Favorite: No
Link: https://attachments.convertkitcdnn2.com/256011/0667f690-8375-4cff-9265-69b86ae8f866/7-Day_Meal_Plan.pdf

Gluten Free
Oat flour is generally gluten-free, but if you have a severe reaction to gluten, double-check.
Replace soy milk with another non-dairy milk alternative.
If making in advance, store the compote separately. The pancakes keep for 2-3 days.

[1]
[20] minutes
[1] serving

[1]
[0.5 cup] oat flour
[0.67 tsp] baking powder
[1.33 tbsp] maple syrup
[0.33 tsp] vanilla extract
[2.67 tbsp] coconut yogurt
[2.67 tbsp] Soy milk
[0.33 cup] blackberries
[0.33 cup] Cherries

Steps:
1. In a small bowl, combine soy milk, yogurt, maple syrup, and vanilla.
2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour and baking powder. Add the liquid ingredients and mix until
3. there are no flour streaks. Allow the batter to rest for about 5 minutes.
4. Heat a non-stick frying pan on medium heat (or use a little bit of cooking spray). Scoop the
5. batter onto the hot pan, using a small ladle or a squeezy bottle. After the edges of the pancake
6. start looking dry, gently pick the edges off the pan with a spatula and flip the pancake. Cook for
7. about a minute on the other side. Repeat with the rest of the batter.
8. To make the blackberry compote, add blackberries and cherries into a small saucepan, along
9. with 2-3 tbsp of water. Heat up on medium heat and cook for about 10 minutes.
10. Stack your pancakes and top them with berry compote. Enjoy!

6. Lay out the tortillas, and evenly divide the chickpea cauliflower mixture, and then lettuce. Top

8. Store in the fridge for up to 4 days.
